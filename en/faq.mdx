## Does SeaBond automatically make money for me?

No. SeaBond is a Hyperliquid trading data and copy trading assistant. You still need to understand trading risk. It should not be treated as a guaranteed-return tool.

## Is the highest-PnL wallet always the best wallet to follow?

No. The highest-PnL wallet may also use higher leverage, take deeper drawdowns, or rely on unusual one-off trades. After opening details, review positions, historical trades, drawdown, and current risk together.

## Why can a strong-looking wallet be unsuitable for copy trading?

PnL is only the result; it does not prove the process is controllable. Very small account size, one outsized win, extreme leverage, frequent averaging down, or a current position with large unrealized profit can all make copy trading risk much higher.

## Why is my copy trading result different from the lead wallet?

Your entry time, capital size, execution price, slippage, latency, and margin state can all differ. A profitable lead wallet does not guarantee your copy trading account will be profitable.

## What is the difference between Smart Copy and Instant Copy?

Smart Copy is more conservative and avoids clearly unfavorable existing-position syncs. Instant Copy is more direct and is better suited to users who already understand the lead wallet's style and can accept current price differences.

## Why are Overview and Copy Trading different in Profile?

Overview shows the total account, including the main wallet and copy trading accounts. The Copy Trading page only shows copy trading accounts.

## When should I stop copy trading?

Reassess the rule if the lead wallet starts losing repeatedly, changes style, increases leverage, or if your copy trading drawdown exceeds your budget.

## What are favorites and labels for?

Favorites and labels help manage candidate wallets. They work like research notes so you can remember why you were watching a wallet.
