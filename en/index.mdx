SeaBond is a trading discovery and copy trading tool built for Hyperliquid users. You can use it to find wallets worth studying, understand how they trade, save promising wallets for follow-up, and manage copy trading exposure separately from your main account.

This documentation does not tell you what to buy or which wallet to follow. It explains what each SeaBond page is used for, what data to check before following a wallet, and how to manage the risk after a copy trading rule is created.

## What you can do with SeaBond

If this is your first time using SeaBond, a typical workflow looks like this:

1. **Discover wallets**: use Smart Money to find wallets with notable trading performance.
2. **Review wallet details**: check current positions, historical trades, drawdown, leverage, and trading style.
3. **Save your research**: add wallets to favorites and use labels to record why they matter.
4. **Create a copy trading rule**: choose the lead wallet, copy mode, and total allocation.
5. **Track the result**: review your account equity, copy trading PnL, margin usage, and active rules in Profile.

## What SeaBond helps answer

- Where should I start if I want to find stable Hyperliquid traders?
- A wallet has high PnL, but was it driven by one lucky trade?
- Does the wallet currently hold oversized positions or use high leverage?
- If I copy trade, where can I see whether the PnL comes from my own trades or from copy trading?
- If a lead wallet changes its trading style, where can I notice it early?

## Three things to know first

<Info>
SeaBond connects wallet discovery, wallet analysis, favorites, labels, and copy trading management in one workflow so you can move from research to execution without losing context.
</Info>

<Warning>
Copy trading is not an automatic profit tool. The lead wallet can lose money, and your account can be affected by volatility, slippage, execution delay, liquidation risk, and leverage.
</Warning>
